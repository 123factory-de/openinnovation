# PitchBook Industry Verticals

> **Source**: [PitchBook — What Are Industry Verticals?](https://pitchbook.com/what-are-industry-verticals)

PitchBook distinguishes between **Industries** (broad sector groups) and **Industry Verticals** (thematic, tech-driven niches that can span multiple industries).

---

## Taxonomy Structure

| Level | Description | Count |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Industry** | Broad sector (e.g., Energy, Healthcare, IT) | 7 |
| **Industry Vertical** | Thematic / tech-driven niche crossing sectors | 50+ |
| **Emerging Space** | Nascent areas, not yet established verticals | 141+ |

**The 7 Primary Industries:**

1. Business Products and Services (B2B)
2. Consumer Products and Services (B2C)
3. Energy
4. Financial Services
5. Healthcare
6. Information Technology
7. Materials and Resources

> **Industry vs. Vertical**: Every company is assigned a primary industry. Verticals are overlay tags for specialized investment themes that cannot be captured by industries alone. A company can belong to one industry and multiple verticals.

## Emerging Spaces (141+)

Nascent areas monitored by PitchBook analysts before they become full verticals. Examples:

- Ghost Kitchens
- Clean Meat / Cultivated Protein
- Space Tourism
- Synthetic Biology
- Carbon Credits Marketplaces
- Nano-satellites
- Mental Wellness Apps

> Emerging Spaces may be promoted to full Verticals as market activity and deal flow grows.

---

## Notes for Classification

1. **One primary industry, many verticals**: A company like ABR (battery recycling) sits in the **Energy** or **Materials and Resources** industry, but may also carry **Cleantech** and **Advanced Manufacturing** verticals.
2. **Verticals are cross-sector**: A cybersecurity firm can be in **IT** or **Financial Services** as its industry, but tagged **Cybersecurity** vertical regardless.
3. **Some verticals are aggregates**: **Industrials** and **TMT** are intentionally broad aggregates; prefer more specific verticals when available.
